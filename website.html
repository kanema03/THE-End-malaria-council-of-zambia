Why End Malaria

Malaria is a threat

Although Zambia has made significant progress against malaria since 2000, it remains a threat to our health and economy.

  • In 2019, there were 5.1 million cases of malaria reported. This is a burden on health facilities, especially as we confront new challenges like COVID-19.
  • Malaria affects vulnerable populations: pregnant women and children. Children under 5 years of age account for 66% of global malaria deaths.
  • Malaria decreases GDP growth by up to 1.3% and causes an estimated 12.8 million days of work to be missed annually (the equivalent of 50,000 full-time workers).
  • Malaria hurts key sectors of the economy (e.g., tourism, agriculture, mining).
  • We have a National Malaria Elimination Strategy, but must overcome a US$100 million budget gap to implement it.

What we do

Advocacy

Ending malaria must remain a strategic priority for all sectors and at all levels (from national down to the community). The End Malaria Council actively sensitises religious and traditional leaders, parliamentarians, and business leaders to the objectives of the National Malaria Elimination Strategy.

Examples:

  • Conducted a national television and radio campaign promoting “Malaria Ends with Me!”
  • Organised community marches in major cities
  • Convened a roundtable discussion with religious leaders from all faiths
  • Conducted workshops with parliamentarians
  • Established provincial and district End Malaria Councils
What we do

Action

We work with the National Malaria Elimination Centre and its partners to implement interventions to prevent malaria.

Case Study:
In 2019, the insecticides needed for the Indoor Residual Spraying campaign in North West Province were stuck in Lusaka. Chief Mumena and First Quantum Mining, members of the Council, quickly arranged for transportation of the insecticides, enabling the campaign to proceed on time and as planned.

What we do

Resource Mobilisation

To succeed, we must ensure that there are adequate resources to fully implement the National Malaria Elimination Strategy. We have established a fund managed by an independent Board of Directors that is responsible for mobilising, managing, and distributing financial and in-kind resources as needed to close budget gaps in the national strategy.

Case Study:
In 2020, the National Malaria Elimination Centre planned a Mass Drug Administration campaign to proactively treat at-risk populations in several high-priority districts. However, there was a US$500,000 budget gap for procuring the medicines. As a result, the End Malaria Fund engaged a number of private sector companies and partners, who donated the necessary resources including a generous contribution of more than US$250,000 from Guilin Pharmaceuticals. As a result, the drugs were procured and several additional districts were protected against malaria.

What we do

Accountability

The End Malaria Council & Fund meet at least quarterly to receive updates on the progress against malaria (including the national malaria scorecard) and status of commitments made by the various sectors. This provides an opportunity for the senior leaders to hold that National Malaria Elimination Centre, partners, and each other mutually accountable for ending malaria in Zambia and for being good stewards of donated resources.

Case Study:
The National Malaria Scorecard shared during a meeting revealed low performance on the preventative treatment of pregnant women against malaria. As a result, the members of the Council engaged in a conversation with the National Malaria Elimination Centre, Minister of Health, and partners to discuss why there was a shortage of key medicines. As a result, a joint taskforce was established and partners provided 2,450 tins of medicines, which were distributed nationwide.

The Council

About

Launched in March 2019, the End Malaria Council is composed of senior leaders drawn from government, the private sector, and civil society. We meet quarterly to receive updates from the National Malaria Elimination Centre on the status of malaria and the challenges it faces. We then engage our respective sectors and constituencies to mobilise commitments for advocacy, action, and resources to solve these challenges.

The Fund

The End Malaria Fund is a non-profit foundation established by the members of the End Malaria Council to support resource mobilisation. The Fund is responsible for mobilising, managing, and distributing in-kind and financial resources to close gaps in the National Malaria Elimination Strategy.

Board of Directors

The Fund is managed by an independent Board of Directors appointed by, and accountable to, the End Malaria Council.

The Zambia EMC secretariat hosted a delegation from Angola. The Angola team had an objective of learning about some of the in-country best practices towards the malaria fight, especially focusing on activities under the Faith Leaders Advocacy for Malaria Elimination (FLAME).
