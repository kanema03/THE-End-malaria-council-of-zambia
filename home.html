The End Malaria Council of Zambia

Malaria must end before 2030

WHY END MALARIA

In 2019, Malaria affected 5.1 million Zambians and cost the economy 12.8 million days of work. Together, we can end malaria in Zambia and Africa at large forever, Malaria is a burden on health facilities especially as we confront new challenges like deadly COVID-19. Malaria affects vulnerable populations such as pregnant women and children. Children under 5 years of age account for 66% of global malaria deaths. Malaria decreases GDP growth by up to 1.3% and causes an estimated 12.8 million days of work to be missed annually (the equivalent of 50,000 full-time workers). Malaria hurts key sectors of the economy (e.g., tourism, agriculture, mining). We have a National Malaria Elimination Strategy, but must overcome a US$100 million budget gap to implement it.

Malaria free Africa

Reaching The Zero malaria target,by 2030

WHAT WE DO

Ending malaria must remain a strategic priority for all sectors and at all levels (from national down to the community). The End Malaria Council actively sensitises religious and traditional leaders, parliamentarians, and business leaders to the objectives of the National Malaria Elimination Strategy We work with the National Malaria Elimination Centre and its partners to implement interventions to prevent malaria.
Case Study:
In 2019, the insecticides needed for the Indoor Residual Spraying campaign in North West Province were stuck in Lusaka. Chief Mumena and First Quantum Mining, members of the Council, quickly arranged for transportation of the insecticides, enabling the campaign to proceed on time and as planned.
